The anatomy of a converting page

Your homepage is not a landing page.

A homepage is a reception desk. It greets everyone and points in six directions. A landing page is a closer, built for one visitor with one promise and one action. Here are the six blocks, in order.

The top of the page has five seconds to answer three questions.

Question 1

What is this?

The headline names the exact thing the ad promised. Not your brand story. The thing itself.

Question 2

Is it for me?

The subhead names who it's for and their situation, so the right person feels seen and the wrong one leaves cheaply.

Question 3

What next?

One button, above the fold, that says exactly what happens when it's pressed.

Six blocks, in order

The page, broken down.

01

Headline matches the ad

If the ad said remortgage, the headline says remortgage. Message match is the single biggest reason pages convert or don't, and it's free to fix.

02

One action, above the fold

No menu, no footer links, no Learn More. The button says what happens: "Book my free call", never "Submit".

03

Proof before promises

Regulation status, reviews, years in practice. Whatever is true and checkable goes here, before the form asks for anything.

04

Outcomes, not features

Not "comprehensive mortgage advice". The exact monthly difference in pounds and a clear yes or no. Specifics make the call feel worth booking.

05

Kill the silent objections

"Will this hurt my credit score?" "Am I too early?" If the page doesn't answer these, the back button does.

06

Ask again, with a short form

Name and one way to reach them. Every extra field costs enquiries. The qualifying questions can wait for the call itself.

Same ad spend. Double the enquiries.

A page converting at 10% instead of 5% halves your cost per enquiry overnight, without a penny more of ad spend. That's why I fix the page before I ever ask a client to raise the budget.
