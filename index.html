The BS

"Your campaign reached 80,000 people this month! Impressions are up 34%!"

The Truth

Reach pays the agency. Enquiries pay you. If a report celebrates impressions and clicks but goes quiet on booked calls and cost per enquiry, that silence is the real report.

The BS

"Just send the ad traffic to your website. It has all the information."

The Truth

A homepage is a reception desk. A landing page is a closer. One visitor, one promise, one action. Most "Google Ads doesn't work" stories are actually "my homepage did a landing page's job" stories.

The BS

"Results are flat? We recommend increasing the monthly budget."

The Truth

Pouring more water into a leaking bucket buys you a bigger puddle. Plug the three leaks first — the click, the page, the follow-up — and the budget you already have starts producing.

The Three Leaks

Your budget isn't too small. It's leaking.

Every broker funnel I audit loses money in the same three places. The line below is your funnel: it dips where the money escapes. Find your dips and the same budget starts producing more booked calls.

I draw this exact line on every audit call.
1

The click

Broad keywords, no negatives, ads that promise everything to everyone. You pay for renters researching, students browsing, and competitors clicking. The wrong people arrive before your page even loads.

The fix: tight intent keywords, ruthless negatives, ads that repel the wrong people on purpose.
2

The page

The ad sends people to your homepage. Six menu links, an "Our Story" section, and no clear next step. The right person arrived, looked for what the ad promised, didn't find it in five seconds, and left.

The fix: one dedicated page per offer. See the anatomy →
3

The follow-up

An enquiry comes in at 7pm. It gets a reply the next afternoon. By then they've spoken to two other brokers. The lead you paid for went cold while it sat in an inbox.

The fix: instant notifications, a same-hour response system, automated reminders.
