The BS / The truth

What the industry says. What's actually true.

I post these on LinkedIn every week. Here are the three that cost brokers the most money.

The BS

"Your campaign reached 80,000 people this month. Impressions up 34%."

The truth

Reach pays the agency. Enquiries pay you. A report that celebrates impressions but goes quiet on booked calls and cost per enquiry — that silence is the real report.

The BS

"Just send the ad traffic to your website. It has all the information."

The truth

A homepage is a reception desk. A landing page is a closer. One visitor, one promise, one action. Most "Google Ads doesn't work" stories are "my homepage did a landing page's job" stories.

The BS

"Results flat? We recommend increasing the monthly budget."

The truth

More water into a leaking bucket buys a bigger puddle. Plug the three leaks first — the click, the page, the follow-up — and the budget you already have starts producing.

The three leaks

Your budget isn't too small. It's leaking.

The line below is your funnel. It dips where the money escapes. Find your dips and the same budget produces more booked calls.

Money entering the funnelBooked calls out
01

The click

Broad keywords, no negatives, ads that promise everything to everyone. You pay for renters researching, students browsing, and competitors clicking. The wrong people arrive before your page even loads.

→ The fix: tight intent keywords, ruthless negatives, ads that repel the wrong people on purpose.
02

The page

The ad sends people to your homepage. Six menu links, an "Our Story" section, and no clear next step. The right person arrived, didn't find what the ad promised in five seconds, and left.

→ The fix: one dedicated page per offer. See the anatomy
03

The follow-up

An enquiry comes in at 7pm. It gets a reply the next afternoon. By then they've spoken to two other brokers. The lead you paid for went cold while it sat in an inbox.

→ The fix: instant notifications, a same-hour response system, automated reminders.
