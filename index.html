The BS / The truth

What the industry says. What's actually true.

I post one of these on LinkedIn most weeks. These three cost brokers the most money.

The BS

"Your campaign reached 80,000 people this month. Impressions up 34%."

The truth

Reach pays the agency. Enquiries pay you. When a report celebrates impressions but says nothing about booked calls or cost per enquiry, that silence is the real report.

The BS

"Just send the ad traffic to your website. It has all the information."

The truth

A homepage greets everyone and points in six directions. An ad click needs one page with one promise and one action. Most "Google Ads didn't work for me" stories are really this.

The BS

"Results flat? We recommend increasing the monthly budget."

The truth

More water into a leaking bucket buys a bigger puddle. Fix the leaks first. The budget you already have usually starts producing on its own.

The three leaks

Your budget isn't too small. It's leaking.

This line is your funnel. It dips where money escapes. Every audit I run finds at least one of these dips, usually two.

Money entering the funnelBooked calls out
01

The click

Broad keywords and no negative list means you pay for renters doing research, students writing essays, and competitors having a look. The wrong people arrive before your page even loads.

→ The fix: tight intent keywords and a negative list that gets ruthless maintenance.
02

The page

The ad promised remortgage advice. The homepage it lands on has a menu, an Our Story section and no obvious next step. Five seconds later that visitor is gone, and so is the money that bought the click.

→ The fix: one dedicated page per offer. Here's the anatomy
03

The follow-up

An enquiry comes in at 7pm and gets answered the next afternoon. By then two other brokers have already called them back. Slow follow-up quietly kills more paid leads than bad ads ever do.

→ The fix: instant notifications and a same-hour response habit, with reminders that nag you until it's done.
